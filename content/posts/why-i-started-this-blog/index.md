This is the first post on this blog, so I should probably begin with the simplest question: why start one at all?

## A Buffer Against Endless Input

As everyone knows, or perhaps does not know, doing a PhD can be a remarkably repetitive and output-starved way to spend one's life. A lot of the work happens quietly: reading, debugging, revising, waiting, failing, trying again, and then discovering that the thing you just tried mostly taught you how little you understood yesterday.

That rhythm is necessary, but it is also easy to get trapped inside it. For me, one thing that separates work from a hobby is the balance between input and output. A good hobby does not only ask you to absorb. It gives you a way to make something, however small, and to see your own thinking take shape outside your head.

Writing this blog is my attempt to build that buffer. It lets me step out of a workday where input is often much greater than output, reorganize what I have learned, and return to research with a slightly clearer mind.

## Why Writing, Especially Now

Another reason is Gen AI.

As AI tools reshape how people work, I increasingly feel that humans need more distinctive, output-oriented hobbies. Otherwise, everything starts to smell a little too AI-flavored, does it not?

Writing a blog is one possible answer. It forces me to choose what I actually think. It asks me to explain, not merely collect. It makes my preferences, confusions, and standards more visible. That kind of visible output feels increasingly important in an age where polished text is cheap, but lived judgment is still expensive.

## What This Blog Will Contain

This site will mainly record what I learn and think about in research. That does not mean vague reflection for its own sake. I want the reader to feel that the time exchanged for a post here was worth it.

So I will try to keep one rule: empty talk does not deserve a place on this blog.

The posts I most want to write are about conclusions, tools, methods, and habits that have survived some contact with practice. In the age of AI, when cheap summaries and confident generalities are everywhere, the things worth keeping are often the things that have been tested, corrected, and used.

## Sharing, Not Preaching

I write to share, not to lecture. I am not qualified to preach. More precisely, I do not think anyone is.

I do not have a deep academic background, nor a serious foundation in writing. I am still learning, carefully and sometimes clumsily. If this blog becomes useful, it will not be because I speak from a high place. It will be because I leave a record of what I tried, what worked, what failed, and what I learned after the failure.

## A Note On Style

The style of this site is inspired by Lilian Weng's personal blog. She is one of the researchers I admire, and her writing has always struck me as clear, careful, and generous. I think imitation is a reasonable place to begin.

Over time, I hope this blog will become more recognizably mine. For now, borrowing a good structure is not a bad way to start walking.

## About Me

Oh, and one more thing.

"My identity is rather complicated. I am a poet."

That line is from the film *Manchurian Tiger*. I am borrowing it here because it is funny, a little absurd, and strangely useful. It is also a good warning label for this blog: some posts will be technical, some will be practical, and some may contain a person trying to stay human while learning how to think.
